Office visit for upper respiratory infection. 42-year-old female presents with nasal congestion, sore throat, and mild cough for 4 days. No fever. No shortness of breath. She has tried over-the-counter decongestants with minimal relief. Past medical history significant for seasonal allergies. Currently takes cetirizine 10 mg daily. No known drug allergies. Vital signs: blood pressure 122/76, heart rate 74, temperature 98.4, respiratory rate 14, oxygen saturation 99% on room air. Physical exam reveals mild nasal mucosal edema, clear posterior pharynx without exudates, lungs clear to auscultation. Assessment: Acute viral upper respiratory infection. Plan: Supportive care, increase fluids, continue antihistamines, may add pseudoephedrine for congestion, return if symptoms worsen or persist beyond 10 days.
