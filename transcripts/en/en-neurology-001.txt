Neurology consultation for new onset seizure. Patient is a 28-year-old female with no prior seizure history who presented to the emergency department after witnessed generalized tonic-clonic seizure lasting approximately 2 minutes with post-ictal confusion for 15 minutes. No tongue bite or incontinence noted. She reports recent sleep deprivation due to work stress, averaging 4 hours of sleep nightly for the past week. She denies alcohol or recreational drug use. No head trauma. Family history notable for cousin with epilepsy. Vital signs stable. Neurological exam: alert and oriented, cranial nerves intact, motor strength 5/5 throughout, sensation intact, reflexes 2+ symmetric, negative Romberg, gait steady. MRI brain without contrast shows no acute abnormality. EEG pending. Labs including comprehensive metabolic panel, CBC, and toxicology screen are unremarkable. Assessment: New onset seizure, likely provoked by sleep deprivation. Plan: EEG to evaluate for epileptiform activity, counsel on seizure precautions including no driving for 6 months per state law, avoid sleep deprivation and excessive alcohol, levetiracetam 500 mg twice daily started as prophylaxis given presentation severity, follow up in clinic in 2 weeks with EEG results, return to ED if recurrent seizure.
