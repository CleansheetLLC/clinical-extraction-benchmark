Procedure note for colonoscopy. Patient is a 55-year-old male undergoing screening colonoscopy. Informed consent obtained. Pre-procedure timeout performed confirming correct patient and procedure. Sedation with propofol administered by anesthesia. Scope inserted through anus and advanced to cecum. Cecal landmarks including appendiceal orifice and ileocecal valve identified. Withdrawal time 8 minutes. Findings: Two sessile polyps identified, one 6 millimeter polyp in the ascending colon and one 4 millimeter polyp in the sigmoid colon. Both polyps removed with cold snare polypectomy and retrieved for pathology. Mucosa otherwise normal throughout. No bleeding complications. Patient tolerated procedure well and transferred to recovery in stable condition. Estimated blood loss minimal. Specimens sent to pathology. Recommendations: Await pathology results, repeat colonoscopy in 3 to 5 years depending on histology, resume normal diet, avoid driving for 24 hours.
